FAQ for Camps

How many pupil's are there in a class?

We only have six spaces on each class - the smallest teacher to student ratio in the UK

Does my child need to bring a laptop

Yes please and with a working web browser installed (we prefer Firefox but Chrome or Safari are fine also). You can hire a laptop from us for a small fee of £5 but there are only a limited number available.

Why aren't the camps day long?

Learning to code requires a lot of energy and a lot of screen time. While we mix the classes up with off-screen activities, crafting, and games we also want to ensure that the pupils get to spend their afternoons in the great outdoors or with family - finding inspiration for their next code creation! Most coding camps that run all day long fill up the time with passive screen time and videos - we prefer to concentrate all our active learning time into a fun filled morning of activities. We do offer gaming afternoons for students on the MineCraft Modding camp - where coders can play MineCraft on a secure server. Click here for more details



Child protection and safety

Every member of staff at DLab (including the Rocket Coder team) has an up to date DBS certificate. We're fully insured and first-aid qualified as well. You can find out more by reading our safeguarding policies here

Can we bring snacks?

Rocket Coders provide free healthy organic vegetarian snacks for coders during their 11 o’clock re-fuelling break. If your children have special dietary requirements you can let us know in the booking form. It's fine to send your children with snacks also but please make sure they contain no nuts!

Is my child assessed?

After the camp your child will receive a certificate, an iron-on coding patch and an assessment with constructive feedback on their coding and creative ability.

Can I see my children’s work?

Yes! Each student on the camp will receive their own page on the Rocket Coders website where they can show off their work to friends, family, and fellow coders.
